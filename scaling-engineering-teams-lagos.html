BUILDING GREAT TECH
TEAMS IN LAGOS.

A simple guide on how to grow a successful team of computer experts while dealing with the unique challenges of Lagos.

Contents
1. The Lagos Tech Scene 2. Solving Light and Internet Problems 3. Finding and Growing Great Workers 4. Working from Home and Office 5. Keeping the Team Strong 6. Building Your App the Right Way 7. What the Future Holds

1. The Lagos Tech Scene

Lagos is the heartbeat of technology in Africa. Many companies that build apps for health, schools, and money start right here. In 2026, it is more exciting than ever, but it is also a place where things change very fast.

Building a team of computer experts (called "engineers") in Lagos is different from building one in London or New York. Here, you don't just have to find the right people; you also have to make sure they have electricity, a good computer, and the right training.

The Challenge: Most schools don't teach exactly what tech companies need. This means many young people need extra training before they are ready for big jobs.

2. Solving Light and Internet Problems

In Lagos, "light" (electricity) and internet can be shaky. If your team spends all day worrying about their battery or their data, they can't focus on building great things.

2.1 Back-up Power is a Must

A professional tech team must have a plan for power. This usually means giving workers "inverters" (big batteries for the house) so they can keep working even when the public light goes off. This helps them stay calm and do their best work.

2.2 Two Internet Sources

One internet connection is never enough. Most experts use two: one fast local fiber and one satellite service like Starlink. If one fails, the other keeps them online.

Pro Tip Use "Cloud Tools." Instead of keeping all work on a laptop, keep it on the internet (the cloud). If a laptop breaks, the work is safe and you can just use another computer to finish.

3. Finding and Growing Great Workers

Finding experts in Lagos is hard because many people move to other countries for work. This is why you must always be looking for new talent.

The 70/30 Rule: Great teams grow 70% of their staff from inside. They hire young, smart people and train them. Only 30% are hired as experts from outside.

When hiring, look for people who are good at solving puzzles and thinking clearly. Tools and languages (like Go, Rust, or TypeScript) change all the time, but a smart brain that can solve problems is useful forever.

4. Working from Home and Office

Everyone knows Lagos traffic is tough. It wastes time and makes people tired. To grow a great team, you should let people work from home most of the time.

4.1 Write it Down

Instead of having many long meetings, successful teams write everything down in simple documents. This is helpful because someone whose light is off can read the notes later and still know exactly what to do. This is called "talking without needing to be there at the same time."

4.2 Meet for Strategy

Only meet in person 1 or 2 days a week. Use this time for big plans and celebrating wins. This keeps the team happy and saves them from the stress of daily traffic.

5. Keeping the Team Strong

It is a fact that some experts will move to other countries (this is called "Japa"). A smart team builds itself so it doesn't fall apart when someone leaves.

  • Write Everything Down: Make sure no secret is kept in just one person's head. If they leave, the team should still know how the app works.
  • Pay Fairly: Pay people based on what global companies pay. Using USD (dollars) as a guide helps protect their money from losing value.
  • Shared Success: Give the team a "piece of the company" (called stock options). When the company wins, they win too.

6. Building Your App the Right Way

As more people use your app, it needs to be built strongly. Imagine building a house that can easily add more rooms without falling down.

6.1 Neat and Organized Code

Don't build everything as one big, messy pile. Build it in neat "blocks" (like LEGOs) so that if you change one part, you don't break the whole thing.

6.2 Watch Your App Carefully

You must always watch how your app is working for real people. In Lagos, some people use old phones or slow internet. Your app should still work smoothly for them. If it's slow, you need to know exactly why so you can fix it.

7. What the Future Holds

Growing a team in Lagos is a big job, but it is very rewarding. It is about more than just writing code; it is about solving real-life problems like light and traffic while helping people grow their careers.

Lagos is full of smart, hard-working people. In the future, more and more global companies will look to Lagos for talent. If you learn how to lead and build teams here, you will be one of the leaders of the new African economy.

Let's talk about the rules. It is very important that your team knows who owns the code they write. This is part of the law. Also, because the value of the Naira can change, many experts want to be paid in a way that keeps their money safe, like using the US Dollar as a guide. This helps them stay focused on work instead of worrying about prices in the market.

In Section 1, we talked about the difference between school and work. At work, you don't just need to know how to "type." You need to understand how the whole computer works. This is what we call "First Principles." We teach students at Sabicrest how to understand these deep rules so they can solve any problem, not just easy ones.

A happy team is a team that finishes work fast. We try to make it so that when a builder writes code, they can see it working in less than 5 minutes. This is hard when the internet is slow, but we use special tricks like "caching" (saving files locally) to make it faster.

Also, many experts who worked at big companies like Google or Amazon are coming back to Lagos. These "Returnees" bring great secrets on how to build things perfectly. Teams should look for these people to help lead and teach the younger ones.

When you add new things to your app, sometimes they might break. We use "Safety Toggles" (called feature flags) to turn off a new part instantly if it causes a problem. This keeps the app safe for everyone while the experts fix the bug.

Don't work alone. Join groups with other tech leaders. Talking to others helps you learn about new laws or better ways to deal with Lagos problems. If people know your company is a helpful part of the community, they will want to work for you.

Finally, as a team grows bigger than 20 people, you need "People Managers." These are leaders who don't just write code; they care for the people. They help with career plans, listen to problems, and make sure no one is too stressed. Lagos life can be stressful, so a kind leader makes a big difference.

In summary, building a team in Lagos is about being smart and resilient. You have to be a bit of a builder, a bit of a teacher, and a bit of a friend. The work is hard, but the chance to build the future of Africa is the best job in the world.
